Understanding Moment of Force

The moment of force (or torque) is a measure of the tendency of a force to rotate an object about an axis. It is calculated as:

Moment = Force × Distance

Where:

  • Force is measured in Newtons (N)
  • Distance is measured in meters (m)
  • Moment is measured in Newton-meters (N⋅m)
Note: The direction of rotation (clockwise or anticlockwise) is determined by the position of the force relative to the pivot point.

Principle of Moments

The principle of moments states that when a system is in equilibrium, the sum of clockwise moments equals the sum of anticlockwise moments about any point.

For a basic lever with weights on both sides:

m₁ × d₁ = m₂ × d₂

Where:

  • m₁ = mass on one side
  • d₁ = distance from pivot to m₁
  • m₂ = mass on the other side
  • d₂ = distance from pivot to m₂

This equation allows you to calculate any one of these values if you know the other three, making it possible to balance a lever system precisely.
